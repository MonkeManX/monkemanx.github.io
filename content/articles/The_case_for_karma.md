For the argument to work two assumptions are needed:
1. There is no uniform self, in other word an elliminative concept of identiy
2. The normative value of external and internal event is dependent in its entirey on the intention of the subejct towards these event i.e. their reaction.

With these two assumption, we can now do an arugment to showcase the law of karma that goes something like this:

```md
1. Momentary Self: There is no single, enduring self; the “I” exists only moment to moment, constantly arising and passing away.
2. Arising and Passing of Consciousness: Each moment of consciousness arises as a new “I” and passes away as it ceases.
3.Habitsbitual Structure: Each arising consciousness carries pre-existing habit patterns (dispositions, tendencies, character traits).
4. Determination by Past Consciousness: These habit patterns are fully determined by the immediately preceding consciousness.
5. External Events are Normatively Neutral: Events in the external world are not inherently good or bad; their value is determined by how the subject reacts to them.
6. Reaction Depends on Habits: How a subject reacts to an external event is determined by the habit patterns of the arising consciousness.
7. Happiness Depends on Reaction: The happiness of a momentary “I” depends on its reactions to external events. Since these reactions are determined by its habits, the current I’s happiness depends on the current habits, which are inherited from past I.
8. Moral Quality of Habits: Good habits lead to beneficial reactions to events and thus to happiness; bad habits lead to harmful reactions and thus to unhappiness.
9. Habits Shape the Future: The habit patterns of future I arise from the current I’s habits and current actions.
10. Responsibility for Future Happiness: Therefore, the happiness of a future I (the next arising of consciousness) depends on the actions of the current I. By cultivating good actions and habits now, one influences the happiness of future incarnations of the self.
```
